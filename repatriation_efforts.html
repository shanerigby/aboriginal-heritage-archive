Repatriation Efforts to Date

An Ongoing Process of Restitution

The fundamental purpose of cataloging and digitizing this private collection is to facilitate the responsible repatriation of these Aboriginal artefacts back to the descendants of their Traditional Custodians.

Institutional Contact

As part of our commitment to returning these items, formal contact was initiated with the Australian Museum. Unfortunately, they were unable to take the items into their collection or facilitate their immediate repatriation. Their institutional mandate and resources actively focus strongly on artefacts originating from within New South Wales (NSW), whereas this collection originates from the Northern Territory, Western Australia, and Queensland.

Note on Additional Archive Items: The Australian Museum is, however, currently considering the provenance and potential acquisition of an Aboriginal breastplate (also known historically as a 'king plate') that remains in this collection. It is engraved with the lettering "BILLE KING OF LONGTON" (or possibly "BILLF"). Documentation and imagery of this artefact will be integrated into this digital archive shortly.

Federal Framework Alignment

While exploring institutional avenues, alignment with apex federal heritage frameworks is critical. The Australian Institute of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Studies (AIATSIS) leads the Return of Cultural Heritage (RoCH) program. Although the RoCH program is definitively tasked with securing the return of cultural material held overseas, AIATSIS's broader mandate of facilitating the return of objects to Aboriginal custodians affirms that returning heritage empowers communities to make active decisions about where and how their materials are best cared for. We have now initiated formal contact with AIATSIS to seek their guidance and explore potential pathways for domestic restitution.

Seeking Community Knowledge

Due to the geographical spread of the collection, identifying and contacting the exact local Native Title bodies, Land Councils, and living descendants for each remote location is a complex ongoing effort.

If you have any particular cultural connection to, or knowledge of any living descendants of the Traditional Custodians of these specific lands, we urge you to get in touch. Any information or introduction that may help facilitate the swift and ethical repatriation of these artefacts is profoundly welcomed.
