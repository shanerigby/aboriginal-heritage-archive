Traditional Custodians: The Ewamian People

About the People

The Ewamian people are the Traditional Owners of the Gulf Savannah region in Far North Queensland. Bound by the Einasleigh and Copperfield rivers, their deep connection to Country is evidenced by extensive rock art galleries and a profound cultural obligation to protect the region's geological and sacred sites.

Positioned in the remote Etheridge Shire of North Queensland, Agate Creek is geologically iconic. Its striking terrain is underlain by the Agate Creek Volcanic Group - an ancient sequence of volcanic activity from the early Permian age. Here, silica-rich solutions permeated cooling basaltic andesite lava flows, filling gas bubbles over millions of years to grow structurally flawless agates. While largely known today as a commercial and recreational gemstone reserve, the entirety of the broader region falls under the custodianship of the Ewamian People, and sporadic stone artefact scatters suggest its peripheral use during early nomadic movements.

A Note on Artefact Protection

Under strict local and federal laws (such as the Queensland Aboriginal Cultural Heritage Act 2003), all Aboriginal archaeological places and objects are now definitively protected. Visitors encountering unknown potential artefacts in the wild must invariably leave them undisturbed and report them to relevant Traditional Owner groups or State Heritage Branches.
